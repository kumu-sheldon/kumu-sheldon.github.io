Ulua

Moku O Keawe, Kona, Kalaoa

This is the first time I caught an Ulua on a surface plug. It measured 39" from nose to tail fork and approximately 40lbs. It was a 15-20 minute fight before I was able to lang it. Normally, I would catch and release this fish, but this Ulua couldn't be revived after the fight... we got lots of good meals out of it.

  • WAHI: Moku O Keawe, Kona, Kalaoa
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 6 Kēkēmapa 2016

Tracks

Oʻahu, Honouliuli, Waimānalo

For several years, this was my daily ritual: surf for a couple hours before work. I lived on Oʻahu, Honouliuli, Kapolei for 3 years and ended up surfing up and down the Waiʻanae and Nānākuli coast almost 7 days a week. Tracks was one of my go-to spots since it was 10 minutes from my house, and 5 minutes to work. Really shallow reef (roughly 4-6ft deep) and loaded with wana, but the waves were so good!

  • WAHI: Oʻahu, Honouliuli, Waimānalo
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 1 Pepeluali 2014

ʻŌmilu

Moku O Keawe, Kona, Kaloko

Healthy little 19.5" (nose to tail fork) ʻŌmilu caught in Kaloko on a peak high tide. I watched this one feeding for about 5 minutes before I tried fishing. Put up a good fight on light tackle gear. Snapped the picture and let um go. Catch and release!

  • WAHI: Moku O Keawe, Kona, Kaloko
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 25 Iulai 2016

Weke ʻUla

Moku O Keawe, Kona, Honokohauiki

Caught this small Weke ʻUla vertical jigging off a boat outside Honokohau Harbor. Steamed topped off with hot peanut oil... ONO!

  • WAHI: Moku O Keawe, Kona, Honokohauiki
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 28 ʻApelila 2018

Ewa

Oʻahu, Honouliuli, Waimānalo

One thing I miss about Oʻahu is the ease at which you can find a surf spot. Standup paddle surfing made it even easier to get away from crowds or surf waves nobody really wants to surf. I wouldn't be able to count the number of times I've scored good waves by myself for hours.

  • WAHI: Oʻahu, Honouliuli, Waimānalo
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 21 Ianuali 2015

Hoe Waʻa

Moku O Keawe, Kona, Hōlualoa

When surf is flat and the fish aren't biting... bring out the waʻa! I love catching the sun peaking over Hualālai in the morning. My typical morning paddle starts at Kamakahonu (Kailua Bay) to Hōlualoa Bay, where I'd rest and catch some waves before going back... roughly 6 miles + surfing.

  • WAHI: Moku O Keawe, Kona, Hōlualoa
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 3 Iune 2017
