ABOUT


ʻŌleʻaʻoʻAha Aloha'Ōlelo i nā hoʻokūkū 'ōlelo Hawaiʻi kahi e paʻi ai nā haumāna a me nā poʻe kūpuna mai nā mokupuni o Hawaiʻi nei i ka kākauʻana i ka'ōlelo Hawaiʻi, ka'ōlelo, ka hoʻomanaʻo, nā mele a me nā mele. 'Āʻawiʻia ka 'Aha Aloha'Ōlelo no keʻano he hui kaiaulu. Ma waho aʻe o ka hoʻokōʻana i nā'ōlelo Hawaiʻi,'Āla Aloha'Ōlelo e hōʻike i nā mele Hawaiʻi ma o ka nui o nā lanakila lanakila ma ka Hōkū Hanohano, a me kaʻai, ka hoʻonaʻauao, keʻano, nā mea kanu,ʻoihana,ʻoihana, hōʻikeʻike, kahi pāʻani keiki aʻoi aku.

“I ʻike ia nō ke kanaka no kekahi lāhui ma kāna ʻōlelo. Inā e nalowale ana ka ʻōlelo makuahine o kekahi lāhui, e nalo hia aku ana nō ia lāhui.”

Ka Puʻuhonua o nā Hawaiʻi, 26 Ianuali 1917



‘Aha Aloha ʻŌlelo features Hawaiian language competitions in which students and adults from around the Hawaiian Islands compete in Hawaiian writing, speech, memorization, chant and singing events. ‘Aha Aloha ʻŌlelo is offered as a free community event. In addition to the Hawaiian language competitions, ‘Aha Aloha ʻŌlelo features Hawaiian music by multiple Nā Hōkū Hanohano Award winners, as well as food, education, fashion, plants, art, crafts, demonstrations, a keiki play zone and more.

The nation to which people belong to is known in the language they speak. If the mother tongue of a nation were to be forgotten, that nation would surely be obliterated.

Ka Puʻuhonua o nā Hawaiʻi, January 26, 1917
