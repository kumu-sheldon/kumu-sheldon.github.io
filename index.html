ABOUT

ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi
Ua inoa ʻia mai ka papahana ʻo Niuolahiki no kekahi kupua uluniu kupaianaha ka mea nāna i lawe i kāna moʻopuna mai Hawaiʻi a i Kahiki. Me ia manaʻo hoʻokahi, lawe pū ʻia kēia papahana ʻōlelo a mauli ola Hawaiʻi e nā papa pūnaewele ʻo Niuolahiki mai Hawaiʻi aku a puni ka honua.

ʻŌlelo Pelekania
Niuolahiki is named for a legendary coconut tree who transports his grandchild from Hawai‘i to a distant land far across the ocean. Likewise, the Niuolahiki Distance Learning Program extends its culturally-rooted Hawaiian language program throughout the world.

Ulua

Kalaoa, Kona, Moku O Keawe

This is the first time I caught an Ulua on a surface plug. It measured 39" from nose to tail fork and approximately 40lbs. It was a 15-20 minute fight before I was able to land it. Normally, I would catch and release this fish, but this Ulua couldn't be revived after the fight... so, we got lots of good meals out of it.

  • KAHI: Kalaoa, Kona, Moku O Keawe
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 6 Kēkēmapa 2016

Tracks

Waimānalo, Honouliuli, Oʻahu

For several years, this was my daily ritual: surf for a couple hours before work. I lived on Oʻahu, in Kapolei for 3 years and ended up surfing up and down the Waiʻanae and Nānākuli coast almost 7 days a week. Tracks was one of my go-to spots since it was 10 minutes from my house, and 5 minutes to work. Really shallow reef (roughly 4-6ft deep) and loaded with wana, but the waves were so good!

  • KAHI: Waimānalo, Honouliuli, Oʻahu
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 1 Pepeluali 2014

ʻŌmilu

Kaloko, Kona, Moku O Keawe

Healthy little 19.5" (nose to tail fork) ʻŌmilu caught in Kaloko on a peak high tide. I watched this one feeding for about 5 minutes before I tried fishing. Put up a good fight on light tackle gear. Snapped the picture and let um go. Catch and release!

  • KAHI: Kaloko, Kona, Moku O Keawe
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 25 Iulai 2016

Hoe Waʻa

Hōlualoa, Kona, Moku O Keawe

When surf is flat and the fish aren't biting... bring out the waʻa! I love catching the sun peaking over Hualālai in the morning. My typical morning paddle starts at Kamakahonu (Kailua Bay) to Hōlualoa Bay, where I'd rest and catch some waves before going back... roughly 6 miles + surfing.

  • KAHI: Hōlualoa, Kona, Moku O Keawe
  • I KA MANAWA HEA: 3 Iune 2017
